# RF-VIRTUALLAB: Analog Communication Virtual Lab 📡

An interactive, web-based virtual laboratory designed to simulate and visualize various analog communication systems, modulation techniques, and hardware behaviors. This platform serves as an intuitive educational tool for students and engineering enthusiasts to explore RF (Radio Frequency) and communication principles in real-time.

🌐 **Live Demo:**
https://rupjitd645-ui.github.io/Rupjit/

---

## 🚀 Features

* **Interactive System Block Diagrams:** Click through the core stages of a communication system—**Transmitter**, **Channel**, and **Receiver**—to inspect internal sub-components and mathematical equations.
* **Virtual Digital Oscilloscope:** Real-time time-domain and frequency-domain visualization of message ($Msg$) and modulated ($Mod$) signals with adjustable timebase and voltage scales.
* **Phasor Diagram / V-Space Analysis:** Integrated vector space visualization to analyze signal phase and magnitude relationships dynamically.
* **Comprehensive Lab Modules:**
    * **Introduction:** General overview & core modulation principles (Antenna size reduction, multiplexing, noise immunity).
    * **Modulation Labs:** DSB-TC (AM), DSB-SC, SSB-SC, and VSB-SC.
    * **Hardware Simulation:** Interactive sections for AM Modulators, SSB Generation, AM Detectors, and Superheterodyne Receivers.
    * **Angle Modulation:** Dedicated FM Laboratory environment.
* **Automated Lab Reporting:** Export your active workbench configurations and simulation results instantly with the **"Export Lab Report"** feature.

## 📈 Core Principles Covered

The platform mathematically simulates and teaches key communication concepts such as:

1. **Antenna Size Reduction:** Visualizing how shifting signals to higher frequencies ($\text{MHz}$ bands) via modulation reduces the physical antenna length ($L \propto \lambda = c/f$) from kilometers to meters.
2. **Multiplexing:** Demonstrating how multiple transmitters share a single physical channel simultaneously via Frequency Division Multiplexing (FDM).
3. **Noise Immunity:** Comparing how different modulation schemes (like AM vs. FM) trade bandwidth for signal integrity against noise.
